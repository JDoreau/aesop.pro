What to expect.

01 Send a short message. You don't need to have it all figured out — a paragraph about your situation and what's not working is plenty. "Our reporting is a mess and nobody trusts the numbers" is a perfectly good starting point.
02 We'll schedule a 30-minute call. No agenda, no presentation. I'll ask questions about your data environment, your team, and what you're trying to accomplish. You'll ask whatever you need to ask.
03 Honest next steps. If there's a clear fit, I'll propose a scoped discovery engagement. If not, I'll tell you why and suggest alternatives. Either way you'll leave the call with something useful.
04 No pressure, no follow-up campaign. If you reach out and decide not to move forward, that's fine. One follow-up if I haven't heard back; after that the ball is in your court.
Is Aesop the right fit?
Good fit
Small to mid-sized companies or nonprofits (10–500 people) that have outgrown spreadsheets
Organizations where a business owner is committed to seeing a project through
Teams that want to understand and own their reporting, not just receive it
Situations where the problem is clear but the solution isn't — yet
Organizations already using Power BI, Tableau, or Snowflake (or evaluating them)
Less ideal fit
Large enterprises needing a team of 10+ consultants — that's not what this is
Projects requiring significant custom software engineering (not BI/reporting work)
Engagements where there's no internal owner accountable for adoption
Organizations that have decided on a solution before understanding the problem
