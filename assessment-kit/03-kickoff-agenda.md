# Reporting Clarity Assessment — Kickoff Agenda & Scope Confirmation

> **Aesop Analytics · TRUST Method, Phase 1 (Triage)**
> The kickoff is a 45–60 minute working session, not a ceremony. By the end, scope is
> confirmed in writing, access is unblocked, interviews are locked, and both sides agree
> on what "done" looks like. Run it with the sponsor and, where possible, the lead analyst.

## Agenda (45–60 min)

### 1. Reconfirm the goal and the trigger (5 min)
Restate, in the sponsor's own words from the diagnostic, why this is happening now.
Confirm it still holds. If the trigger has shifted since signing, surface it now —
it changes where to focus.

### 2. Walk the scope and deliverables (10 min)
Read through the 10 deliverables together. For each, confirm it's in scope and note any
the client cares about most. Name the exclusions explicitly — what this Assessment is
*not* (no dashboard building, no production data work, no tool procurement).

### 3. Confirm the environment boundary (10 min)
Agree on exactly which reports, tools, teams, and data domains are in scope. A reporting
estate can sprawl; the fixed fee depends on a bounded estate. Write the boundary down.
- In scope: ______________________________________________
- Explicitly out of scope: ________________________________

### 4. Access and logistics (10 min)
Walk the intake access log. Resolve anything outstanding with an owner and a date.
Confirm the interview schedule and the list of interviewees.

### 5. How we'll work (5 min)
- Cadence: a short weekly check-in + async updates.
- One point of contact each side for day-to-day questions.
- AI-use and data-handling: state plainly how AI-assisted workflows are used (research,
  synthesis, documentation, QA) and that Jonathan reviews and owns all findings. Confirm
  this matches the client's data policy.

### 6. The readout, defined (5 min)
Agree the date, the audience (who's in the room), and the format (executive readout +
full deliverable package). Confirm the acceptance criteria — what makes this "accepted
and complete."

### 7. Next steps & first actions (5 min)
Assign the immediate actions with owners and dates. End the call with everyone knowing
their next move.

## The 2–3 week shape (share with client so expectations are set)

A typical rhythm. Adjust to the engagement, but the client should know what each week holds.

**Week 1 — Triage & discovery**
Kickoff, stakeholder interviews, reporting inventory begun, access confirmed. By end of
week: a working picture of what exists and a refined trust hypothesis.

**Week 2 — Reconcile & analyze**
Metric conflict mapping, source-of-truth tracing, manual-burden and fragility analysis,
dashboard adoption review, governance gap assessment. By end of week: findings drafted,
quick-wins identified.

**Week 3 — Synthesize & deliver**
Prioritize into the 30/60/90 roadmap, build the executive readout, finalize the
deliverable package, present. Define next steps — implementation, or the client acting
on quick-wins independently.

> If scope is small (beta / nonprofit / tightly-scoped), this compresses to ~2 weeks.
> If the estate is larger than the boundary assumed, that's a scope conversation — not
> silent extra work.

---

## Kickoff close — what success looks like at the readout

Reaffirm the promise so everyone is anchored to the same outcome:

> **By the end of this engagement, leadership will know exactly which reports exist,
> which can be trusted, where definitions conflict, what's authoritative, where the
> manual burden is — and a prioritized 30/60/90-day plan for what to fix first.**
