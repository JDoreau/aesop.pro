# Reporting Clarity Assessment — Engagement Intake Checklist

> **Aesop Analytics · TRUST Method, Phase 1 (Triage)**
> Use this checklist between signed SOW and kickoff. The goal is to arrive at the
> kickoff call already knowing the shape of the environment, so the 2–3 week clock
> is spent on analysis and synthesis — not chasing access.

**Client:** _______________________  **Engagement dates:** ____________  **Sponsor:** ____________

---

## How to use this

Send the *client-facing request list* (Section A) to the sponsor immediately after
signing. Complete the *internal preparation* (Section B) yourself. Anything still
open at kickoff goes on the kickoff agenda as an action item with an owner and a date.

Each item notes which final deliverable it feeds, so nothing is collected "just in case."

---

## Section A — Client-facing request list

Send this to the executive sponsor. Frame it as "what helps us move fast," not an audit demand.

### A1. People & access

- [ ] **Stakeholder list** — names, roles, and which reports/decisions each one touches. Feeds: *Stakeholder Pain Summary, deliverable 2.*
- [ ] **Interview scheduling** — 3–6 interviews booked (see interview guide for who). 30–45 min each.
- [ ] **Read access to reporting tools** — Power BI / Tableau / Looker workspace(s), at viewer level minimum. Feeds: *deliverables 1, 6.*
- [ ] **Access to shared report storage** — the network drives, SharePoint, or folders where recurring spreadsheets live. Feeds: *deliverables 1, 5.*
- [ ] **A point of contact for access issues** — someone who can grant or escalate access within 24h.

> **Scope guardrail:** Request *viewer/read* access only. The Assessment is diagnostic.
> Aesop does not need edit rights, admin rights, or production database credentials at
> this stage. If a client offers broad access, decline the parts not needed — it protects
> both sides and reinforces the fixed-scope promise.

### A2. Documents & artifacts (whatever exists — "none" is a valid and useful answer)

- [ ] Any existing **report catalog or inventory**, even a partial spreadsheet.
- [ ] Any **KPI definitions or metric glossary** currently in use. Feeds: *deliverables 3, KPI Conflict Map.*
- [ ] **Board deck or executive reporting pack** from the last 1–2 cycles. Feeds: *deliverables 2, 6.*
- [ ] Any **data dictionary, ERD, or source documentation** that exists. Feeds: *deliverable 4, Source-of-Truth Map.*
- [ ] Org chart for the teams involved in or consuming reporting.
- [ ] Any prior **audit, assessment, or consultant report** on the data/reporting environment.

### A3. Context the sponsor should send in writing

- [ ] **The trigger** — what prompted them to seek help now? (Board pressure, a specific dispute, a migration, a leadership change?)
- [ ] **The decisions that feel harder than they should be.**
- [ ] **The numbers people argue about most.**
- [ ] **What "success" looks like** for this engagement in the sponsor's own words.

---

## Section B — Internal preparation (Aesop-side)

Complete before kickoff. None of this requires the client.

- [ ] **SOW re-read** — confirm the agreed scope boundaries, the deliverable list, exclusions, and the acceptance criteria. Note anything ambiguous to raise at kickoff.
- [ ] **Vertical brief** — 30 minutes on the client's industry reporting norms (e.g. healthcare census/enrollment metrics, RevOps pipeline definitions, fund/property NOI). Know the language before the first interview.
- [ ] **Client workspace created** — one controlled workspace for this engagement, with AI-use and data-handling rules set per the SOW.
- [ ] **Deliverable templates staged** — copies of the inventory, scorecard, KPI conflict map, source map, and roadmap templates, pre-labeled with the client name and engagement dates.
- [ ] **Interview guides personalized** — swap in the actual stakeholder names/roles and any sector-specific KPI language.
- [ ] **Working trust hypothesis drafted** — based on the diagnostic call, write a one-paragraph hypothesis of where trust is likely breaking down. This is a hypothesis to test, not a conclusion. Revisit it at the readout.

## Section D — Readiness gate (before kickoff)

Proceed to kickoff only when these are true. If two or more are unmet, the engagement
start may need to shift — flag it to the sponsor early rather than starting half-blind.

- [ ] At least the executive sponsor and one report builder interview are scheduled.
- [ ] Read access to the primary BI tool is granted or scheduled within 48h of kickoff.
- [ ] The sponsor has named the trigger and at least one disputed number.
- [ ] Scope and deliverables are confirmed and unambiguous.
